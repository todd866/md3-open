# Shock Management

## Classification of Shock

Shock is defined as inadequate tissue perfusion leading to cellular hypoxia. Classification is based on the underlying mechanism of circulatory failure.

<KeyPoint title="Shock Types">
**Q:** The four main categories of shock are [___], [___], distributive, and obstructive.

**A:** hypovolaemic; cardiogenic
</KeyPoint>

<KeyPoint title="Distributive Shock">
**Q:** The most common cause of distributive shock in the hospital setting is [___].

**A:** sepsis
</KeyPoint>

<KeyPoint title="Obstructive Shock">
**Q:** The three classic causes of obstructive shock are cardiac tamponade, tension pneumothorax, and massive [___].

**A:** pulmonary embolism
</KeyPoint>

## Haemodynamic Patterns

Different shock types produce distinct haemodynamic profiles that guide diagnosis and treatment.

<Mnemonic title="Shock Haemodynamics">
- **Hypovolaemic** — low CO, high SVR, low CVP, low PCWP
- **Cardiogenic** — low CO, high SVR, high CVP, high PCWP
- **Distributive (septic)** — high CO, low SVR, low CVP, low PCWP
- **Obstructive** — low CO, high SVR, high CVP, variable PCWP
</Mnemonic>

<KeyPoint title="Cardiogenic vs Hypovolaemic">
**Q:** The key haemodynamic difference between cardiogenic and hypovolaemic shock is that cardiogenic shock has [___] filling pressures (CVP and PCWP).

**A:** elevated
</KeyPoint>

## Vasopressor Therapy

<KeyPoint title="First-Line Vasopressor">
**Q:** The first-line vasopressor for septic shock (after fluid resuscitation) is [___].

**A:** noradrenaline
</KeyPoint>

<KeyPoint title="Vasopressin">
**Q:** Vasopressin is used as a second-line agent in septic shock at a fixed dose of [___] units/min to reduce noradrenaline requirements.

**A:** 0.03
</KeyPoint>

<ClinicalPearl>
**Q:** In cardiogenic shock with adequate blood pressure but poor cardiac output, the inotrope of choice is [___], which increases contractility via beta-1 receptor agonism.

**A:** dobutamine
</ClinicalPearl>

## Monitoring Adequacy of Resuscitation

<KeyPoint title="Lactate Clearance">
**Q:** A serum lactate level greater than [___] mmol/L in the context of suspected infection meets the Sepsis-3 criterion for septic shock (along with vasopressor requirement).

**A:** 2
</KeyPoint>

<Danger title="Over-Resuscitation">
**Q:** Excessive fluid administration in shock increases mortality. The clinical sign of fluid overload in the lungs is [___] oedema, visible as bilateral white-out on chest X-ray.

**A:** pulmonary
</Danger>

<KeyPoint title="MAP Target">
**Q:** The minimum mean arterial pressure (MAP) target during resuscitation of septic shock is [___] mmHg.

**A:** 65
</KeyPoint>
