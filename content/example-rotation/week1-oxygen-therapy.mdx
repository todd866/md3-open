# Oxygen Therapy

## Physiology of Oxygen Delivery

Oxygen delivery to tissues depends on cardiac output, haemoglobin concentration, and oxygen saturation. The oxygen-haemoglobin dissociation curve describes the relationship between partial pressure of oxygen (PaO2) and haemoglobin saturation (SaO2).

<KeyPoint title="Oxygen Delivery">
**Q:** The three determinants of oxygen delivery (DO2) are cardiac output, [___], and [___].

**A:** haemoglobin concentration; oxygen saturation
</KeyPoint>

<KeyPoint title="Dissociation Curve">
**Q:** A rightward shift of the oxygen-haemoglobin dissociation curve is caused by increased [___], decreased pH, or increased temperature.

**A:** 2,3-DPG
</KeyPoint>

## Oxygen Delivery Devices

### Low-Flow Devices

Low-flow devices provide supplemental oxygen at flow rates below the patient's inspiratory demand. The delivered FiO2 varies with respiratory rate and tidal volume.

<KeyPoint>
**Q:** Nasal cannulae deliver approximately [___]% FiO2 per litre of oxygen flow (up to 6 L/min).

**A:** 4
</KeyPoint>

<Mnemonic title="Low-Flow Devices">
- **Nasal cannulae** — 1-6 L/min, FiO2 24-44%
- **Simple face mask** — 5-10 L/min, FiO2 40-60%
- **Non-rebreather mask** — 10-15 L/min, FiO2 60-90%
</Mnemonic>

### High-Flow Devices

<KeyPoint>
**Q:** High-flow nasal cannulae (HFNC) can deliver flows up to [___] L/min with precise FiO2 control.

**A:** 60
</KeyPoint>

<ClinicalPearl>
**Q:** The mechanism by which HFNC reduces work of breathing (beyond supplemental O2) is by providing [___] pressure, which acts like low-level CPAP.

**A:** positive end-expiratory
</ClinicalPearl>

## Oxygen Toxicity

Prolonged exposure to high FiO2 (above 0.6) can cause absorption atelectasis, oxidative damage to the alveolar epithelium, and retinopathy of prematurity in neonates.

<Danger title="Oxygen Toxicity">
**Q:** In patients with chronic CO2 retention (e.g., severe COPD), excessive oxygen therapy can worsen hypercapnia by reducing [___] drive and increasing V/Q mismatch.

**A:** hypoxic respiratory
</Danger>

<KeyPoint title="Target Saturations">
**Q:** For patients at risk of hypercapnic respiratory failure, the target SpO2 range is [___] to [___]%.

**A:** 88; 92
</KeyPoint>
