# Fluid Resuscitation

## Physiology of Fluid Balance

Total body water comprises approximately 60% of lean body mass in adults. This is distributed between intracellular (two-thirds) and extracellular (one-third) compartments.

<KeyPoint title="Fluid Compartments">
**Q:** The intracellular fluid compartment contains approximately [___] of total body water.

**A:** two-thirds
</KeyPoint>

<KeyPoint>
**Q:** Plasma oncotic pressure is primarily maintained by [___], which accounts for approximately 80% of colloid osmotic pressure.

**A:** albumin
</KeyPoint>

## Crystalloids vs Colloids

<KeyPoint title="Normal Saline">
**Q:** The chloride concentration of 0.9% normal saline is [___] mmol/L, which is significantly higher than plasma chloride (normal 95-105 mmol/L).

**A:** 154
</KeyPoint>

<Mnemonic title="Crystalloid Composition">
- **Normal saline (0.9% NaCl)** — Na 154, Cl 154, osmolarity 308
- **Hartmann's solution** — Na 131, Cl 111, K 5, Ca 2, lactate 29
- **Plasmalyte** — Na 140, Cl 98, K 5, Mg 1.5, acetate 27, gluconate 23
</Mnemonic>

<ClinicalPearl>
**Q:** Large-volume 0.9% saline resuscitation can cause hyperchloraemic metabolic [___] due to excess chloride load.

**A:** acidosis
</ClinicalPearl>

## Assessment of Volume Status

Clinical assessment of dehydration correlates poorly with measured fluid deficits. No single sign is reliable in isolation.

<KeyPoint>
**Q:** The most specific clinical sign of hypovolaemia in adults is a postural drop in systolic blood pressure greater than [___] mmHg.

**A:** 20
</KeyPoint>

<Danger title="Fluid Overload">
**Q:** Administering excessive IV fluids can cause pulmonary oedema. The lung finding most specific for fluid overload on chest X-ray is [___] vascular redistribution (cephalisation).

**A:** upper lobe
</Danger>

## Resuscitation Targets

<KeyPoint>
**Q:** In septic shock, the Surviving Sepsis Campaign recommends an initial fluid bolus of [___] mL/kg crystalloid within the first 3 hours.

**A:** 30
</KeyPoint>

<ClinicalPearl>
**Q:** After initial resuscitation, fluid responsiveness can be assessed dynamically by measuring a change in stroke volume or cardiac output with a [___] leg raise.

**A:** passive
</ClinicalPearl>
